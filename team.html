Our Team

Building the future of sports innovation from Cambridge

We're a team connecting research, emerging technology, and real-world sport. Our work sits at the intersection of ideas and execution: turning concepts into pilots, and ambition into systems that actually function.

Joseph Keady

Founder & CEO

Joseph Keady is the founder and CEO of Future Faster Sports, a Cambridge-based sports innovation ecosystem designed to translate world-class research into real-world performance and commercial impact.

Born in Cambridge, Joseph has a background spanning sports business, entrepreneurship, and innovation ecosystem development, with experience working across the UK, Europe, and international markets.

Through Future Faster Sports, he is building a platform that connects researchers, founders, athletes, and institutions to pilot, validate, and scale emerging technologies using sport as a high-performance testbed. His work sits at the intersection of sport, science, and venture creation, with a focus on long-term ecosystem impact.

Omar Belal

Chief Operating Officer

Omar is co-building Future Faster Sports, a Cambridge-based sports innovation studio connecting research, emerging technology, and real-world sport. His work sits at the intersection of ideas and execution: turning concepts into pilots, and ambition into systems that actually function.

With a background in Politics & International Relations, Omar is driven by how institutions evolve, how power moves, and how change really happens. He's especially interested in spaces where disciplines collide: sport, science, technology, and culture.

At Future Faster Sports, Omar builds bridges between people who rarely speak the same language: researchers, engineers, founders, coaches, and organizations—creating the operational infrastructure that allows innovation to move from theory to practice.

Michael Russum

Tech Lead

Michael Russum is a Software Engineer and AI/ML Developer currently studying for an MSc in Artificial Intelligence. As Tech Lead at Future Faster Sports, he is building the digital infrastructure that powers the sports innovation ecosystem.

With expertise spanning software engineering, artificial intelligence, and machine learning, Michael is also an Assistive Technology Founder, bringing a unique perspective to how technology can enhance human performance and capability.

At Future Faster Sports, Michael leads the development of the technical platforms, data systems, and AI-driven tools that enable researchers, athletes, and entrepreneurs to collaborate, innovate, and scale breakthrough technologies.

Part of Innovate Cambridge

Future Faster Sports is a recognised supporter and member of Innovate Cambridge, the city's flagship initiative for aligning academia, industry, entrepreneurs, and government around the region's future innovation strategy.

We are currently part of The Glasshouse Initiative, a new Innovate Cambridge programme positioning emerging companies within a collaborative, cross-sector innovation environment.
